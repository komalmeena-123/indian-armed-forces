The Garud Commando Force is the special forces unit of the Indian Air Force. It was formed in September 2004 and has a current strength of over 1500 personnel. The unit derives its name from Garuda, a divine bird-like creature in Indian history.

Garud is tasked with the protection of critical Air Force bases and installations; search and rescue during peace and hostilities and disaster relief during calamities. Presently, Garuds are deployed in Congo as part of the UN peace keeping operations.

It was formed in September , 2004 , after an attack on air bases in J&K.

Originally named as “Tiger Force” but later it was changed to “Garud Commando Force”

Gallantry Awards

The Garuds have actively participated in counter-terrorist operations and have received many accolades which include 1 Ashok Chakra, 4 Shaurya Chakras, and many Vayu Sena Medal .

Sergeant Milind Kishor (2018, posthumous).
